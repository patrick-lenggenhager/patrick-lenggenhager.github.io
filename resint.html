Research Interests

My main research interests revolve around topological band theory and materials. I am studying various properties of topological bandstructures, such as momentum space degeneracies (band nodes), topological invariants and their relation to symmetries. In particular, I am interested in a multi-band perspective on the above topics. My work encompasses both working with simple toy models illustrating and/or demonstrating novel topological phenomena as well as simulating/modeling real materials to study possibly observable effects such as transport signatures.

I have just finished up a larger project about triple (nodal) points, which are degeneracies of three bands in momentum space. We have completed the missing classification of those band nodes in the absence of spin (which applies to systems where spin-orbit interactions are negligible but also to many other systems such as photons, phonons, magnons including may metamaterials) and have found a universal higher-order bulk-boundary correspondence as well as relationships to multiband topology. For more information, check out the project description.

I have also been working on implementing and investigating models of condensed matter on hyperbolic lattices, that is lattices in negatively curved space. In contrast to positively curved spaces, such as the surface of a sphere, the negative counterpart cannot be embedded in three-dimensional space. However, we have implemented and measured a hyperbolic lattice in electric circuits, thereby establishing circuits as a platform to study interesting models on hyperbolic lattices. On the theory side, we have studied fundamental models of topological band insulators on hyperbolic lattices. For more information, check out the project description.

More recently, I have become interested in applying quantum information theoretic tools such as entanglement entropy and mutual information to study condensed matter systems.

Besides these main parts of my research I am also interested in other topics, on some of which I have previously worked while others might lead to future projects:

  • Interplay of non-Abelian topology with interactions
  • Transport and non-equilibrium physics / Floquet systems (Master thesis)
  • Information theory and statistical physics (see this paper)
  • Field theoretical methods in condensed matter theory
